» Information

From Friday 13th to Tuesday 17th of November the annual Tryton unconference comes to Buenos Aires. We warmly welcome users, developers and people interested to learn more about Tryton. Discover Trytons features first hand and learn about the latest technical developments presented in the upcomming version 3.8.

Attending the Tryton unconference is at no charge, but we expect donations to make this event possible. We recommend a donation of 300 ARS which can payed on-the-spot.

» Traveling

The City of Buenos Aires has two main airports: Ministro Pistarini Ezeiza International Airport (EZE), located at about 34 km from Buenos Aires, and Aeroparque Jorge Newbery Domestic Airport (AEP), which mainly handles domestic flights, and is located on the margins of Río de la Plata, close to downtown Buenos Aires. When travelling from abroad, you will arrive in Ezeiza Airport. To get out of Ezeiza Airport, you have these options: Taxi cabs or “remise” services. You are advised to take them at the booths within the main hall. Estimate cost: US$55 Shuttle Bus. You will be able to pay the fare upon arrival or book it in advance at Manuel Tienda Leon’s The ride takes about 1.15 hours and makes two stops: Puerto Madero – downtown Buenos Aires and Jorge Newbery Domestic Airport. Bus Ride Fare: US$15 approx. You are advised to take any of the transportation options offered in the official booths within the main hall, not the ones offered by individuals.

Once in the city, the Buenos Aires Underground, locally known as Subte, is the best way to get around
